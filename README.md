**📋 Survey Progress Dashboard — Description & Insights

**Overview
This dashboard tracks survey enumeration progress across 5 Nigerian states, providing a breakdown of respondents by state and Local Government Area (LGA). The total surveys completed stands at 1,947.

Tool: PowerBi

🌍 State-by-State Insights
1. Katsina — 467 (Leading State)

Highest survey count overall
Top LGA: Katsina LGA itself with 107 — the single highest LGA count in the entire dataset
Other notable LGAs: Kaita (85), Charanchi (74), Daura (77)
Insight: Katsina is the best-performing state with strong and relatively spread enumeration across its LGAs

2. Zamfara — 447 (2nd Place)

Very close to Katsina, strong performance
Top LGA: Gusau (117) — actually the highest single LGA count across all states
Other LGAs: Maru (86), Kaura Namoda (75), Bakura (56)
Insight: Zamfara's performance is driven significantly by Gusau; other LGAs have moderate contributions

3. Kebbi — 444 (3rd Place)

Nearly tied with Zamfara
Top LGA: Birnin Kebbi (116) — second highest LGA count overall
Other LGAs: Argungu (70), Kalgo (72), Dandi (82)
Insight: Like Zamfara, Kebbi's numbers are heavily anchored by one dominant LGA (Birnin Kebbi)

4. Adamawa — 336 (4th Place)

Noticeably behind the top three states
Top LGA: Yola South (75)
Other LGAs: Fufore (68), Mubi South (58), Mubi North (51)
Insight: Adamawa shows a more even spread across LGAs but lacks a dominant high-performing LGA to boost totals

5. Taraba — 253 (Lowest)

Significantly trails the other four states
Top LGA: Bali (44), Gassol (49)
All LGAs are below 50, with most in the 32–49 range
Insight: Taraba is the most underperforming state — no single LGA has a standout count, suggesting systemic challenges in survey reach or field operations


⚠️ Expected Enumeration Gap
The Expected Enumeration section shows 0 values for all five states (Adamawa, Katsina, Kebbi, Taraba, Zamfara).
Insight: This is a critical flag — either:

Targets/quotas have not been set for this survey exercise, or
The expected data has not been entered into the system

Without targets, it is impossible to measure completion rate or efficiency. Setting benchmarks per state and LGA should be a priority.

📊 Distribution Analysis
The donut chart shows a relatively balanced distribution among three states:

Katsina, Zamfara, and Kebbi are nearly equal (~23% each)
Adamawa and Taraba together account for only ~30% of total surveys

Insight: There is a two-tier performance gap — the top 3 states vs. the bottom 2 states. Taraba in particular, at just 13%, needs urgent operational support.

✅ Summary of Key Takeaways
#Insight1Gusau (Zamfara) and Birnin Kebbi (Kebbi) are the top-performing LGAs nationally2Taraba is critically behind — all its LGAs are below 50 respondents3No enumeration targets are set, making performance measurement impossible4Adamawa needs a boost — its spread is even but collectively insufficient5Total of 1,947 surveys completed — overall progress depends on undefined targets

📌 Recommendations

Set expected enumeration targets per state and LGA immediately to enable progress tracking
Deploy additional field officers to Taraba to close the performance gap
Investigate what's working in Katsina and Gusau and replicate strategies in underperforming areas
Consider mid-survey check-ins to course-correct before the enumeration window closes
